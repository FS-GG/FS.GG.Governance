# Feature Specification: The Design-System Adapter — A Second, Unrelated Domain Adopts The Kernel From Fixtures

**Feature Branch**: `011-adapter-designsystem`

**Created**: 2026-06-18

**Status**: Draft

**Input**: User description: "create specs for the next item in the project." (resolved to F11 · `011-adapter-designsystem` from the dated Spec Kit implementation plan)

**Change Classification**: **Tier 1** — introduces the **second concrete production adapter**: the **design-system adapter**, the domain whose vocabulary is adherence to a *design language* (the worked example is Ant Design) — design tokens, colour/contrast policy, spacing/control-height scales, rendered captures, interaction-state and page-pattern specs. It supplies — through the already-shipped F09 SPI — *only* its own facts (selected policy, design rules, deterministic verdicts, recorded reviews, blockers), an artifact mapping (`DesignArtifactRef → ArtifactRef`), the probes its rules compose (`surfaceMatches`, `contrastMeets`, …), a **tiered rule catalog**, and the fences naming its high-stakes surfaces; everything cross-cutting is reused from the kernel (F01–F07). It adds a new curated public signature contract and a new surface-area baseline. It is a **pure** value/fold layer run against a **fixture token tree** — the sensing of a live design system (reading the token tree, capturing rendered output, computing contrast ratios) is the F08 effects shell / F12 CLI's job, not this feature — so **Constitution Principle IV is N/A** (no state, no I/O). It is the second of the two unrelated domains that meet **Milestone M3 — the adoption bar**: a domain that shares **none** of the Spec Kit adapter's (F10) vocabulary or shape adopts the same kernel cheaply.

## User Scenarios & Testing *(mandatory)*

The "users" of this feature are the **maintainers of a product built on a design language** (an Elmish / FS.GG.Render product using Ant Design) who want to govern adherence to that language, and, one level down, the **kernel itself** as the observer attached to that design surface. The design system is the **subject**; governance is the **observer**: it never authors or regenerates tokens, captures, or specs — it reads them as artifacts, asserts facts, and runs rules. The anti-goal ("governance is not a design tool — it does not generate tokens or rendered output") is therefore **structural, not aspirational**: the adapter has no authoring or rendering surface at all, only a sensing/checking one, and it carries **no rendering vocabulary** into the generic kernel.

This feature is the **second concrete adopter** of the F09 adapter SPI, and the one that makes the adoption bar real: where F10 (the Spec Kit adapter) governs a **staged lifecycle** with phases-as-facts, a phase guard, and a single merge fence, F11 governs a **flat design surface** with no lifecycle at all — and it does so **without copying domain #1's shape**. It supplies **exactly** its own five components — a closed `DesignSystemFact` union (selected policy, design rules, deterministic verdicts, recorded reviews, blockers), a `DesignArtifactRef → ArtifactRef` mapping, the probes its rules compose, the **tiered rule catalog**, and the fences over its high-stakes surfaces — and gets inference, three-valued verdicts, the reified `Check` algebra and its interpreters, `CheckTier` arbitration, the evidence/taint DAG, JSON explanation and contract, severity, and routing/run-modes **for free**. The adapter re-implements **none** of those. That two domains as different as a Spec Kit lifecycle and a design language plug into one unchanged kernel — each with its own vocabulary, neither shaped like the other — is the evidence that the abstraction sits at the right altitude.

The keystone behaviours are five, each a direct translation of the design-system adapter design (`docs/governance-design/adapters.md`): **(1)** a second, unrelated domain adopts the kernel by supplying **only** the five SPI components plus the F04 `Bridge` wiring — no phases, no `whenPhase`, no merge fence, none of domain #1's machinery — proving generality is real, not assumed; **(2)** the catalog assigns each rule a `CheckTier` (*who decides*) and a `Severity` (*whether it blocks*) — the deterministic, contract-bearing token/contrast/surface checks are the few that **block**; the visual-judgement rules use the `Opaque` hatch, which keeps them out of the `Deterministic` tier and routes them to an agent whose prompt is the rule's `Question`; adopting a **new** design policy is `HumanOnly`; **(3)** the adapter runs entirely against a **fixture token tree** — a few JSON/RON files, no rendering dependency — so it is testable without any consumer repo and **no rendering vocabulary leaks into generic code**; **(4)** every deterministic rule **renders to a sentence and hashes** stably, so what the published contract *advertises* is byte-for-byte what `eval` *enforces* (drift-proof by construction, and the F04 agent-review cache key does not move); and **(5)** the adapter **lifts unchanged** into a project coproduct and composes alongside the **Spec Kit adapter (F10)** at one root — standalone and lifted `(verdict, provenance)` are identical (the F09 faithful-lift guarantee), and the two unrelated domains coexist without either knowing about the other.

This feature builds directly on **F09** (it is authored against the `Adapter<'fact,'artifact,'change>` SPI record and the `Lift`/`Composition` machinery — it supplies the five components and the F04 `Bridge` kernel wiring, and composes at a root alongside F10) and through F09 on **F07** (its rules carry `CheckTier`/`Severity`, and its fences over high-stakes surfaces drive `Route.route`), **F05** (a token or verdict resting on a synthetic/unmeasured input is `AutoSynthetic` and the taint flows by the kernel's own fixed point), **F04** (each rule is a `CheckRule<DesignSystemFact>` — a `Check` + `CheckTier` + `Severity`), **F03** (its probes are reified `Check`s over `Probe`/`ArtifactRef`/`Outcome`), and **F01–F02** (the `Rule`/`FactSet`/`FixedPoint` and the verdict algebra). It is **pure** — values and total folds, no `Model`/`Msg`/`Effect`, no interpreter — so Principle IV does not apply; the impure effects shell (**F08**, already shipped) and the **F12** CLI are what **sense** a live design system into `DesignSystemFact`s and wire the adapter into a running loop. It depends on **F09 only** — never on F10 (the two adapters are independent siblings).

### User Story 1 - A second, unrelated domain governs a design language from the five SPI components — without copying domain #1's shape (Priority: P1)

A maintainer adopts the kernel for a design language by supplying **only** the five SPI components for the design-system domain: (1) the closed `DesignSystemFact` union (selected policy, design rules, deterministic verdicts, recorded reviews, blockers), (2) the `DesignArtifactRef → ArtifactRef` mapping (`TokenDocument`, `GeneratedTokenSurface`, `RenderedCapture`, `InteractionStateSpec`, `PagePatternSpec` onto the kernel's structural artifact reference), (3) the probes its rules compose (`surfaceMatches`, `contrastMeets`, …), (4) the tiered rule catalog, and (5) the fences over its high-stakes surfaces. Inference, arbitration, evidence, rendering, hashing, explanation, severity, and run-modes are **all reused from the kernel** — the adapter writes none of them. Crucially, this domain shares **nothing** with the Spec Kit adapter (F10): no `Phase`, no `whenPhase`, no lifecycle-as-facts, no merge fence. Generality is demonstrated by *difference*, not by a second copy of the first adapter.

**Why this priority**: This is the thesis of the milestone made concrete for domain #2 — the kernel is a library, not a platform, only if a domain that looks **nothing like** the first one can adopt it just as cheaply. If governing a design language required reusing Spec Kit's phase machinery, or re-implementing inference/arbitration, the abstraction would be at the wrong altitude and the adoption bar would be unmet. The five-part design-system adapter is the minimum viable second adopter and the foundation every other story in this feature tiers, renders, or lifts, so it ships first.

**Independent Test**: Author the design-system adapter as a single `Adapter<DesignSystemFact, DesignArtifactRef, _>` value supplying only the five components; feed it a synthetic `FactSet` of `DesignSystemFact`s drawn from the fixture token tree and confirm it derives facts, evaluates its rules, and renders an explanation using **only** kernel facilities — and that the adapter module contains **no** inference, arbitration, evidence, rendering, hashing, explanation, severity, or routing code of its own, **no** phase/lifecycle machinery copied from F10, and **no** function that authors a token, a capture, or a spec.

**Acceptance Scenarios**:

1. **Given** the F09 adapter SPI, **When** the design-system adapter supplies the five components (the `DesignSystemFact` union, the `DesignArtifactRef` mapping, probes, the tiered catalog, the high-stakes fences) plus the F04 `Bridge` kernel wiring, **Then** a design language is fully governable with no further domain code.
2. **Given** the design-system adapter, **When** its rules are evaluated over a supplied `FactSet`, **Then** every cross-cutting facility (fixed point, tier arbitration, verdict algebra, evidence/taint, render/hash/explain, routing) comes from the kernel, not the adapter.
3. **Given** both shipped adapters, **When** the design-system adapter is inspected, **Then** it shares **no** vocabulary or shape with the Spec Kit adapter — no `Phase`, no `whenPhase`, no merge fence — and depends on the SPI and kernel only, never on F10.

### User Story 2 - The tiered catalog: deterministic token/contrast checks block, visual-judgement rules route to an agent, policy adoption is human-only (Priority: P1)

The catalog assigns each rule a tier (*who decides*) and a severity (*whether it blocks*). The **deterministic, contract-bearing** rules are the few that block: **token drift** (the generated token surface matches the source token document), **colour/contrast policy** (a WCAG / Ant ratio is met), and the **token surface gate** (the public token surface is the blessed one). Other deterministic rules — **spacing scale**, **control-height defaults**, **intent coverage** (a declared intent is consumed), **visual-state resolution** — are advisory. The **visual-judgement** rules — "does the rendered control match the spec intent?", the "four values" (natural / certain / meaningful / growing), page-pattern correctness, "colour as information, not decoration", motion restraint, elevation/overlay layering — use the `Opaque` hatch, which automatically keeps them out of the `Deterministic` tier and routes them to an **agent reviewer** whose prompt is the rule's `Question`. Adopting a **new** design policy (e.g. switching to Material) is `HumanOnly` and blocking — a person owns that decision, and it never resolves to a deterministic verdict.

**Why this priority**: The tier split is what makes a design language governable at all — most adherence questions are matters of visual judgement that a deterministic engine must **not** pretend to answer, while a handful (token currency, contrast, public surface) are exact contracts that should block. Routing each question to whoever is competent to answer it (engine / agent / human) is the whole elegance of the `CheckTier` model, and the catalog is the surface the rest of the feature renders, routes, and lifts, so it is co-equal P1 with the adapter itself.

**Independent Test**: Evaluate the deterministic token-drift and contrast rules over fixture facts and confirm they produce definite `Pass`/`Fail` verdicts and are `Blocking`; evaluate an `Opaque` judgement rule (e.g. "colour-conveys-information") and confirm it is `AgentReviewed`, never resolves deterministically, and carries the `Question` that becomes the agent's prompt; evaluate the `HumanOnly` policy-adoption rule and confirm it routes to a person and stays undecided by the engine.

**Acceptance Scenarios**:

1. **Given** the deterministic token-drift, contrast-policy, and token-surface-gate rules, **When** they are evaluated over fixture facts, **Then** they produce definite verdicts and are the `Blocking` rules of the catalog.
2. **Given** an `Opaque`/`AgentReviewed` judgement rule, **When** it is evaluated, **Then** it stays out of the `Deterministic` tier and routes to an agent reviewer whose prompt is the rule's `Question` — it never resolves to a deterministic `Pass`/`Fail`.
3. **Given** the `HumanOnly` "adopt a new policy" rule, **When** it is evaluated, **Then** it routes to a person and never resolves to a deterministic verdict, exactly as the tier demands.

### User Story 3 - The adapter runs against a fixture token tree — no rendering dependency, no rendering vocabulary in generic code (Priority: P1)

The whole adapter is exercised against a tiny **fixture token tree** — a few JSON/RON files representing a token document, a generated token surface, an interaction-state spec, and a page-pattern spec — with **no** dependency on any rendering engine and **no** consumer repo. The probes (`surfaceMatches generated canonical`, `contrastMeets policy surface`) read those fixture artifacts as data and report `Met`/`Unmet`/`Unknown`. Critically, **no rendering vocabulary** (no token type, no colour model, no layout concept) appears in the **generic kernel or SPI** — all of it lives behind the adapter's closed `DesignSystemFact`/`DesignArtifactRef`, so removing the adapter removes the design vocabulary entirely and the kernel stays domain-neutral.

**Why this priority**: Running from fixtures with no rendering dependency is the practical proof that this domain adopted the kernel *cheaply* and that the kernel stayed *generic*. If the adapter needed a live renderer to be tested, or if design vocabulary leaked into the kernel, the boundary would be in the wrong place — the kernel would be a design tool, not a domain-neutral engine. It is co-equal P1 because it is the constraint that keeps both the adapter testable and the kernel deletable-down-to-neutral.

**Independent Test**: Point the adapter at the fixture token tree, supply the corresponding `DesignSystemFact`s, and confirm the full catalog evaluates and explains with **no** rendering library on the path; then inspect the kernel and SPI surfaces and confirm **zero** rendering/token/colour vocabulary appears outside the adapter's closed types.

**Acceptance Scenarios**:

1. **Given** the fixture token tree (JSON/RON files, no renderer), **When** the catalog is evaluated over the facts drawn from it, **Then** every rule produces a verdict and an explanation with no rendering dependency on the path.
2. **Given** the kernel and SPI public surfaces, **When** they are inspected, **Then** **no** rendering, token, colour, or layout vocabulary appears in them — all design vocabulary is confined to the adapter's closed `DesignSystemFact`/`DesignArtifactRef`.
3. **Given** the probes `surfaceMatches`/`contrastMeets`, **When** they read fixture artifacts, **Then** they report a three-valued `Outcome` (`Met`/`Unmet`/`Unknown`) over the fixtures as data — they do not render, capture, or author anything.

### User Story 4 - Every deterministic rule renders to a sentence and hashes stably — advertised equals enforced (Priority: P2)

Each deterministic rule in the catalog **renders to a sentence** (e.g. "surface-matches(GeneratedTokenSurface, TokenDocument)") and **hashes** to a stable, canonical value through the kernel's interpreters. The `Statement` published in the contract is literally `Check.render` of the same value `eval` ran, so what the contract **advertises** and what the engine **enforces** cannot diverge — drift-proofness is structural, not a convention. The hash canonicalizes commutative nodes (so `allOf [a; b]` and `allOf [b; a]` hash equal) while keeping positional nodes positional, so the F04 agent-review cache key for the judgement rules is stable across re-orderings and does not cause spurious re-review.

**Why this priority**: Render-and-hash is the property that makes the catalog *explainable* and the agent-review caching *correct* — without it, the published contract could lie and every cosmetic re-ordering would invalidate cached reviews. It refines the catalog (P1) into a self-describing, cache-stable contract, so it ranks P2 but ships with the catalog because every rule must render and hash to be admitted to it.

**Independent Test**: For 100% of the catalog, confirm each rule renders to a non-empty human-readable sentence and explains itself through the kernel's interpreters; confirm a deterministic rule's hash is stable across commutative re-ordering of its sub-checks and that the published `Statement` equals `Check.render` of the evaluated value; confirm two structurally-equal `Opaque` judgement rules produce the same cache key.

**Acceptance Scenarios**:

1. **Given** any rule in the catalog, **When** it is rendered and explained, **Then** it produces a human-readable sentence and an explanation whose root verdict equals `eval` — advertised equals enforced.
2. **Given** a deterministic rule combining sub-checks commutatively, **When** its hash is computed, **Then** the hash is invariant under re-ordering of the commutative members (and positional nodes stay positional).
3. **Given** a judgement (`Opaque`) rule, **When** its agent-review cache key is computed, **Then** the key is stable for structurally-equal checks, so cosmetic changes do not force re-review.

### User Story 5 - The adapter lifts unchanged and composes alongside the Spec Kit adapter at one root (Priority: P2)

The design-system adapter governs a design language **standalone** (its rules over `DesignSystemFact` directly) and also **lifts unchanged** into a project coproduct — when a real product uses both a design language **and** Spec Kit, the two adapters compose at a single composition root via a coproduct fact type (`ProjectFact = Design of DesignSystemFact | SpecKit of SpecKitFact | …`), each adapter's catalog lifted into it through the F09 `Lift`/`Composition` machinery. The lift is **semantics-preserving**: a lifted rule's `(verdict, provenance)` and its `Check.render`/`hash`/`reads`/`isReified` are byte-for-byte identical to the standalone original (the F04 agent-review cache key does not move). The two unrelated domains coexist without either knowing about the other; removing one is dropping one `Lifted` from the `compose` list.

**Why this priority**: The faithful lift is the F09 guarantee made concrete for domain #2, and composing the design-system adapter alongside the Spec Kit adapter is the **literal definition of the adoption bar** — two unrelated domains adopting the kernel cheaply and coexisting at one root. It ranks P2 because the standalone adapter, its tiered catalog, and the fixture discipline (P1) are the spine; this story is the proof that the spine composes without surprise.

**Independent Test**: Evaluate every catalog rule standalone over `DesignSystemFact`s and again lifted over coproduct-wrapped facts at a root that also carries the Spec Kit adapter (or a second neutral toy domain), and confirm the `(verdict, provenance)`, render, hash, and reads are identical for 100% of the catalog; confirm the design-system adapter references only the SPI and the kernel, never F10.

**Acceptance Scenarios**:

1. **Given** the design-system adapter, **When** each rule is evaluated standalone and again after lifting into a `ProjectFact` coproduct, **Then** the `(verdict, provenance)` is byte-for-byte identical for 100% of the catalog (the F09 faithful-lift guarantee).
2. **Given** a project that composes the design-system adapter **and** the Spec Kit adapter at one root, **When** the composed rules are evaluated and routed, **Then** the two unrelated domains coexist — neither references the other, and dropping one `Lifted` removes that domain cleanly.
3. **Given** the lifted catalog, **When** a lifted rule's `Check.render`/`hash`/`reads`/`isReified` are computed, **Then** they equal the standalone original's, so the agent-review cache key does not move under composition.

### Edge Cases

- **Generated surface drifts from source**: when `surfaceMatches GeneratedTokenSurface TokenDocument` reports `Unmet`, the token-drift rule is a definite `Fail` (`Blocking`) — distinguishable from `Unknown` when a fixture artifact is missing.
- **Contrast fixture missing or unreadable**: a `contrastMeets` probe over an absent fixture is `Unknown` (undecided), never a silent `Pass` — a missing input is malformed, not compliant.
- **Judgement rule with no agent available**: an `Opaque`/`AgentReviewed` rule that cannot be reviewed stays `Unknown` (undecided) and advisory — it never collapses to a deterministic `Pass`/`Fail`, and the routing/sensing of the agent review is the F08/F12 job, not this feature.
- **Adopting a new design policy**: the `HumanOnly` policy-adoption rule is blocking and never resolves by engine or agent — it routes to a person and stays undecided until a human decides.
- **Synthetic / unmeasured input**: a deterministic verdict (e.g. a contrast result) that rests on a synthetic or unmeasured input is `AutoSynthetic` by the kernel's F05 taint closure, and the always-blocking evidence-honesty rule (`evidence-measured`) — which forbids a deterministic verdict resting on synthetic-tainted evidence — fails at a fenced change; the taint flows by the kernel's fixed point, not adapter code.
- **Commutative re-ordering**: re-ordering the members of an `allOf`/`anyOf` in a deterministic rule does not change its hash (commutative canonicalization), so no spurious cache miss or re-review.
- **Rendering vocabulary leak**: any token/colour/layout type appearing in the kernel or SPI surface is a defect — all design vocabulary must stay behind the adapter's closed `DesignSystemFact`/`DesignArtifactRef`; the surface-drift test guards this.
- **Adapter used alone vs. composed**: the design-system adapter governs a product standalone and also lifts unchanged into a project coproduct alongside the Spec Kit adapter (F10) at one root — the standalone and lifted verdicts are identical (the F09 faithful-lift guarantee).
- **Default posture is advisory**: most of the catalog is advisory by default; only the deterministic token-drift, contrast-policy, token-surface-gate, and evidence-measured rules and the `HumanOnly` policy-adoption rule are `Blocking` (five in all) — a long blocking list would be the design smell of over-fencing.

## Requirements *(mandatory)*

### Functional Requirements

- **FR-001**: The feature MUST define the **design-system domain vocabulary** as a closed `DesignSystemFact` union covering — at minimum — the selected design **policy**, the domain's **design rules**, **deterministic verdicts** (e.g. token-drift / contrast results read from the surface), **recorded reviews**, and **blockers** — the facts the kernel folds over to govern adherence to a design language. The union is owned by this adapter, never copied from or shared with the Spec Kit (F10) domain.
- **FR-002**: The feature MUST provide the **artifact mapping** `DesignArtifactRef → ArtifactRef` — every design artifact kind (`TokenDocument`, `GeneratedTokenSurface`, `RenderedCapture`, `InteractionStateSpec`, `PagePatternSpec`) onto the kernel's structural artifact reference (a `Kind` + a stable `Key`) — the single point where design artifacts meet the otherwise domain-neutral algebra.
- **FR-003**: The feature MUST be authored as a single F09 `Adapter<DesignSystemFact, DesignArtifactRef, _>` value supplying **exactly the five SPI components** (fact-union identity, artifact mapping, probes, rule catalog, fences) plus the F04 `Bridge` kernel wiring — and **nothing more**; everything cross-cutting (inference, arbitration, verdicts, evidence/taint, render/hash/explain, severity, routing/run-modes) MUST be reused from the kernel, not supplied by the adapter.
- **FR-004**: The adapter MUST be an **observer, not an author**: it MUST expose **no** operation that generates, edits, renders, or rewrites a design artifact (a token document, a generated token surface, a rendered capture, a spec). Governance reads artifacts as data and asserts facts; the anti-goal ("governance is not a design tool") is structural, not aspirational.
- **FR-005**: The feature MUST adopt the kernel **without copying domain #1's shape**: it MUST NOT introduce a phase/lifecycle, a `whenPhase` guard, a merge fence, or any Spec Kit (F10) machinery, and MUST NOT reference the Spec Kit adapter. Generality MUST be demonstrated by a domain that differs from domain #1, not by a second copy of it.
- **FR-006**: The feature MUST express adherence checks as a **tiered rule catalog**, each rule a `CheckRule<DesignSystemFact>` carrying a `CheckTier` (*who decides* — `Deterministic`/`AgentReviewed`/`HumanOnly`) and a `Severity` (*whether it blocks*), and each able to **render to a sentence and explain itself**.
- **FR-007**: The catalog MUST include the **deterministic blocking** checks — **token drift** (`surfaceMatches GeneratedTokenSurface TokenDocument`), **colour/contrast policy** (`contrastMeets`), the **token surface gate** (public token surface), and **evidence honesty** (`evidence-measured` — no deterministic verdict rests on a synthetic/unmeasured input; the F05 taint realization, always blocking, no flag flips it) — the **deterministic advisory** checks — spacing scale, control-height defaults, intent coverage, visual-state resolution — the **`AgentReviewed` advisory** judgement checks via the `Opaque` hatch carrying a `Question` (rendered control matches spec intent, the "four values", page-pattern correctness, colour-as-information, motion restraint, elevation/overlay layering), and at least one **`HumanOnly` blocking** check (adopting a new design policy). The catalog therefore has **fifteen** rules in all.
- **FR-008**: The deterministic rules MUST be **reified down to probes** so they render and hash; the **judgement** rules MUST use the `Opaque` hatch, which keeps them out of the `Deterministic` tier and makes them route to an agent reviewer whose prompt is the rule's `Question`. An `Opaque`/`HumanOnly` rule MUST NOT resolve to a deterministic `Pass`/`Fail`.
- **FR-009**: The feature MUST provide a set of **fences** naming the domain's **high-stakes surfaces** (the public token surface / token-surface gate) so the kernel's routing can recognise a high-stakes change; the default posture MUST be **advisory**, with only the deterministic token/contrast/surface rules, the always-blocking evidence-honesty rule (`evidence-measured`), and the `HumanOnly` policy-adoption rule `Blocking` (five rules in all).
- **FR-010**: The adapter MUST be exercised entirely against a **fixture token tree** — a small set of non-rendering fixture artifacts (JSON/RON) — with **no rendering dependency**; the probes MUST read those fixtures as data and report a three-valued `Outcome` (`Met`/`Unmet`/`Unknown`).
- **FR-011**: **No rendering vocabulary** (token types, colour models, layout concepts) MUST appear in the generic kernel or the SPI surface; all design vocabulary MUST be confined to the adapter's closed `DesignSystemFact`/`DesignArtifactRef` so that removing the adapter removes the design vocabulary entirely.
- **FR-012**: Each rule in the catalog MUST **render to a human-readable sentence** and **explain itself** through the kernel's interpreters, and the published `Statement` MUST be literally `Check.render` of the value `eval` ran — so what is advertised and what is enforced cannot diverge.
- **FR-013**: Deterministic rules combining sub-checks commutatively MUST **hash to a canonical value** invariant under re-ordering of commutative members (positional nodes stay positional), so the F04 agent-review cache key is stable across cosmetic re-orderings and does not force spurious re-review.
- **FR-014**: The design-system adapter MUST be **independent and composable**: it references only the F09 SPI and the kernel (never another adapter), it governs a design language standalone, and it MUST **lift unchanged** into a project coproduct when composed with a second domain (the Spec Kit adapter, F10) at one root — the standalone and lifted `(verdict, provenance)`, render, hash, and reads MUST be identical (the F09 faithful-lift guarantee).
- **FR-015**: The adapter MUST be **pure** — values and total functions, no I/O, no `Model`/`Msg`/`Effect`, no interpreter (Constitution Principle IV is N/A). **Sensing** a live design system into `DesignSystemFact`s (reading the token tree, capturing rendered output, computing contrast ratios, hashing artifact content) is the job of the F08 effects shell and the F12 CLI, **not** this feature; tests feed facts drawn from the fixtures directly.
- **FR-016**: The feature MUST live in a **new component** `FS.GG.Governance.Adapters.DesignSystem` separate from the kernel, the SPI, and the Spec Kit adapter, depend on the **SPI** (and through it the kernel) without either depending on it, and keep its dependency footprint **light** (BCL + `FSharp.Core` + SPI + kernel only — no rendering library, no heavy dependency).
- **FR-017**: The public surface introduced by this feature MUST be declared in a **curated signature contract** (`.fsi`) and the API **surface-area baseline MUST be added/updated** to include it (per the repository's surface-drift discipline; Tier 1), with a dependency-hygiene assertion that the adapter references only BCL/`FSharp.Core`/SPI/Kernel (and not F10).

### Key Entities *(include if feature involves data)*

- **`DesignSystemFact` (domain fact union)**: The design-system domain's closed, private vocabulary — selected policy, design rules, deterministic verdicts, recorded reviews, blockers — the facts the kernel folds over to govern adherence to a design language. Owned by this adapter, never copied from or shared with the Spec Kit domain.
- **`DesignArtifactRef`**: The artifact kinds the domain inspects (`TokenDocument`, `GeneratedTokenSurface`, `RenderedCapture`, `InteractionStateSpec`, `PagePatternSpec`), mapped onto the kernel's structural `ArtifactRef` (a `Kind` + stable `Key`).
- **Probes (`surfaceMatches`, `contrastMeets`, …)**: The atomic, inspectable predicates the rules compose, each naming the artifacts it reads; they read fixture artifacts as data and report a three-valued `Outcome`. They never render, capture, or author.
- **Tiered rule catalog**: The reified rules — deterministic blocking (token drift, contrast policy, token surface gate, evidence-measured), deterministic advisory (spacing, control-height, intent coverage, visual-state), `AgentReviewed` judgement via `Opaque`+`Question` (rendered-matches-intent, four values, page-pattern, colour-as-information, motion restraint, elevation/overlay), and `HumanOnly` blocking (adopt a new policy) — fifteen rules in all, each a `CheckRule<DesignSystemFact>` carrying a tier, a severity, and a renderable sentence.
- **High-stakes fences**: The fences naming the domain's high-stakes surfaces (the public token surface / token-surface gate) so the kernel's routing recognises a high-stakes change; advisory is the default, the blocking set stays short.
- **Fixture token tree**: A small, non-rendering set of fixture artifacts (JSON/RON token document, generated surface, interaction-state and page-pattern specs) the adapter is exercised against — no rendering dependency, no consumer repo.

## Success Criteria *(mandatory)*

- **SC-003**: The full catalog evaluates and explains against the **fixture token tree** with **no** rendering library on the path, and inspection of the kernel and SPI surfaces finds **zero** rendering/token/colour/layout vocabulary outside the adapter's closed types (FR-010/FR-011).
- **SC-004**: For **100%** of the catalog, each rule renders to a non-empty human-readable sentence and explains itself with a root verdict equal to `eval`, and the published `Statement` equals `Check.render` of the evaluated value — advertised equals enforced (FR-012).
- **SC-005**: A deterministic rule's hash is **invariant** under commutative re-ordering of its sub-checks (positional nodes stay positional), and two structurally-equal `Opaque` judgement rules produce the **same** agent-review cache key — so cosmetic changes force no spurious re-review (FR-013).
- **SC-006**: The design-system adapter **lifts unchanged** into a project coproduct — for **100%** of its rules, the lifted rule's `(verdict, provenance)`, render, hash, and reads over coproduct-wrapped facts are byte-for-byte identical to the standalone original (the F09 faithful-lift guarantee), and it composes alongside the Spec Kit adapter at one root with neither domain referencing the other (FR-014).
- **SC-007**: The **adoption bar is met** — two unrelated domains (Spec Kit, design-system) adopt the same unchanged kernel, each with its own vocabulary and shape, neither shaped like the other, and removing either is dropping one `Lifted` from the composition (FR-005/FR-014).
- **SC-008**: The new component adds **no heavy dependency** (references only the SPI, the kernel, `FSharp.Core`, and the BCL — no rendering library), neither the kernel nor the SPI nor the Spec Kit adapter references it, and the API surface-area baseline is recorded — the adapter is exercised entirely through the built component, not private helpers (FR-016/FR-017).
